Balikpapan

Signature Dishes

Amplang

If you visit Balikpapan City, Amplang is a type of souvenir that tourists must buy. Amplang is a type of cracker that has a typical Kalimantan fish taste and aroma. Amplang is made from fish meat. Fish that commonly used are belida or flat fish, snakehead fish (haruan) and mackerel fish. The method for making amplang is fairly simple, the ingredients needed are finely ground fish then recovered from sago flour and some complementary spices.

Abon Kepiting

If Solo is famous for its floss beef, Balikpapan is known for its floss crab. In fact, this food is one of the specialties of the area. It is not surprising that crab is one of the marine assets of Balikpapan. If floss is usually made from beef or chicken, this particular one is made from crab meat. Even though I never taste it before, my friends keep saying that it's tasty and delicious to eat with warm white rice. Seafood enthusiast certainly be curious about the taste. The way to cook it is quite tricky. Separating the crab meat from its shell and its claws is the most complicated process. Well, after mashed the meat then given spices such as spices including garlic, onion, and coconut milk to add to the savory taste. Finally,the crab ready to be fried until the color turn to gold brown.

Bingka Kentang

Balikpapan's bingka cake is one of the famous souvenir besides abon kepiting. This cake is the same type with mud cake, but it's way more subtle. There are several types of bingka cakes, there are potato bingka cakes, banana bingka cakes, egg bingka cakes and chocolate bingka cakes. Among all these flavoured cake, potato bingka cake is the most famous. The taste and texture of the potatoes are very thick and add special "wow" taste to your tounge. Lots of bingka cakes that are sold, but the champion for me is the potato bingka cake sold by H. Amin in the Semayang Harbor area.

Pisang Gapit

Banana that combined with brown sugar with a legit taste, people said. This dish is similar to the typical Makassar dish which is pisang epe, but the sauce is different. The existence of pisang gapit rarely found now, but don't worry there are several locations that still exist. One of them is in Waroeng Banjar Bahari, reportedly to be the origin / pioneer of pisang gapit, according to the news i've read. Proven until now that many travelers have been hunting for this culinary treat while exploring Balikpapan. One of the secret ingredient of pisang gapit lies in the jackfruit-flavored brown sauce which is made from brown sugar, coconut milk and jackfruit slices.
